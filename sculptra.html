How Sculptra Works

Sculptra is not a traditional filler. It is a collagen stimulator. While hyaluronic acid fillers like Juvederm add volume directly, Sculptra uses poly-L-lactic acid — a synthetic version of a substance your muscles naturally produce — to trigger your body's own collagen production.

Over the weeks following treatment, new collagen forms beneath the skin, gradually restoring the underlying structure that aging has diminished. The effect is a fuller, more youthful appearance that builds naturally and lasts significantly longer than standard dermal fillers.

Sculptra vs. Traditional Fillers

Sculptra

Stimulates your body's own collagen production using poly-L-lactic acid. Results develop gradually over several weeks. Lasts more than two years. Works as a volumizer and structural rebuilder rather than a wrinkle filler.

HA Fillers (Juvederm, Restylane)

Deliver hyaluronic acid directly into the skin for immediate volume. Results are visible right away. Typically last 6 to 18 months depending on the product. Best for targeted filling of specific lines and contours.

Many patients benefit from both. Your provider can design a plan that combines Sculptra's long-term structural rebuilding with targeted HA filler for areas that need immediate correction.

Your Treatment, Start to Finish

Step 1 — Consultation

Your provider evaluates your areas of volume loss, discusses your goals, and determines whether Sculptra — alone or combined with other treatments — is the right approach. You will leave with a clear understanding of what to expect and a personalized treatment schedule.

Step 2 — Treatment

A topical numbing cream is applied before a series of targeted injections in the treatment area. The material used in Sculptra has been used in plastic and reconstructive surgery for more than 20 years. Most sessions are completed in 30 to 45 minutes.

Step 3 — Recovery and Results

Most patients return to normal activities immediately. Mild swelling and bruising may occur and typically resolve within a few days. You will massage the treated area for five minutes, five times daily, for five days. Results develop gradually over several weeks as collagen rebuilds. Full results last more than two years.

Common Questions

How does Sculptra work?
Sculptra uses poly-L-lactic acid to stimulate your body's own collagen production. Rather than filling wrinkles directly, it rebuilds the underlying structure of your skin over time, creating gradual, natural-looking volume restoration.
How long do results last?
Sculptra results can last more than two years. Because the treatment stimulates your own collagen, the improvement develops gradually and lasts significantly longer than traditional hyaluronic acid fillers.
How many sessions do I need?
Most patients need 2 to 3 treatment sessions spaced several weeks apart. Your provider at Auveau will recommend a schedule based on the degree of volume loss and your goals.
What areas can Sculptra treat?
Sculptra treats all areas of the face including around the eyes, lips, nasolabial folds, cheeks, chin, and neck. Off-label uses include the decolletage, buttocks, elbows, and knees.
Is there downtime?
Most patients return to normal activities immediately. Mild swelling and bruising may occur and typically resolve within a few days. Post-treatment massage is recommended: five minutes, five times daily, for five days.
How is Sculptra different from Juvederm?
Juvederm uses hyaluronic acid to fill lines with immediate results. Sculptra uses poly-L-lactic acid to stimulate your body's own collagen over time. Sculptra results are more gradual but last significantly longer — up to two years or more.
