How a HydraFacial Works

The HydraFacial uses a patented double-helix vortex wand that works in two directions at once. One chamber draws out dead skin cells, dirt, and oil. The other pumps your skin full of nourishing ingredients — hyaluronic acid, vitamin C, and growth factors.

The result is deeper exfoliation and better hydration than a traditional facial, delivered without the abrasion, redness, or recovery that comes with more aggressive resurfacing treatments.

Your Treatment, Step by Step

Step 1 — Cleanse and Exfoliate

A water-based solution is delivered under pressure to break up and lift away the topmost layer of dead skin cells. The spiral suction tip provides gentle, thorough exfoliation without abrasion.

Step 2 — Extract and Peel

A mild chemical peel solution is applied to deepen the exfoliation. Suction extracts debris from pores — more comfortably and effectively than manual extraction.

Step 3 — Infuse and Protect

Customized treatment serums — selected for your skin's needs — are delivered directly into the skin under pressure. The result is deep hydration, visible plumping, and an immediate glow. Your provider finishes with broad-spectrum sunscreen.

Common Questions

What is a HydraFacial?
A medical-grade hydra-dermabrasion treatment that uses patented vortex technology to cleanse, extract, and hydrate skin in one session. It combines physical exfoliation, chemical exfoliation, and nutrient infusion for immediate visible results.
How long does a HydraFacial take?
Sessions are offered in 30, 60, and 90 minute formats depending on the level of customization and add-ons selected. Your provider will recommend the right session length for your goals.
Is there any downtime?
Little to none. You may notice slight flushing for a few hours due to increased blood flow — that is normal and temporary. You can return to your day immediately with a fresh, hydrated glow.
Who is a good candidate?
HydraFacials work on all skin types, including very sensitive skin. The treatment is highly customizable, so your provider can adjust the protocol to match your skin's specific needs and concerns.
How often should I get a HydraFacial?
Most patients benefit from monthly treatments to maintain results. Your skincare professional at Auveau will recommend a schedule based on your skin's needs and goals.
What if I need deeper resurfacing?
The HydraFacial is on the gentler end of the treatment spectrum. If you need more intensive resurfacing, your provider may recommend medical-grade chemical peels or laser treatments instead.
